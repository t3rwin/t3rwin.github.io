2-Channel EEG Neurofeedback System

Tim ErwinMarch 2024

Background

Electroencephalography (EEG), is a method of recording the electrical activity of the brain, typically with non-invasive scalp electrodes. The electrical signals captured via the electrodes are due to the postsynaptic potentials of neurons in the brain, and can be analyzed based on frequency composition and the specific brain regions associated with the originating activity. Mental states can also be inferred from the frequency composition of the brain activity. The goal of this project is to develop an EEG system that provides live feedback regarding the user’s levels of relaxation and concentration. After quantifying the user’s mental state, audio and/or visual feedback can be shown in order to aid in the process of learning how to gain awareness and control of the desired mental states. This process of providing live feedback to a user based on the real-time monitoring of brain activity is known as neurofeedback, which is sometimes used as a supplement when treating things such as focus issues or anxiety. 

The frequencies present in EEG are categorized into the following bands: delta (less than 4 Hz), theta (4-8 Hz), alpha (8-13 Hz), beta (13-30 Hz), and gamma (30-100 Hz). Each of the brainwave frequency ranges represent a specific physiological function, and by measuring the relative power of the frequency ranges, changes in mental states can be quantified. The key frequency bands of interest for this system are theta, alpha, and beta. Beta frequencies, associated with increased cognitive activity and concentration, are measured during neurofeedback sessions aimed towards relaxation. Theta frequencies are linked to deep relaxation and meditation, and alpha frequencies represent alertness and peacefulness. One of the most popular neurofeedback techniques for stress reduction training is the alpha/theta protocol, where a session’s goal is to increase the theta levels relative to alpha.

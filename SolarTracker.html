Solar Tracker

Tim ErwinAUG 4, 2020

Overview

I designed a dual axis solar tracker (light follower) that uses four photoresistors (LDR) to detect the position of a light source. The logic for controlling the motor direction is done by several comparators. An H-bridge and PWM generator drive the DC motors. 

I designed and 3D printed a mechanism that allows my circuit to control one motor in order to follow a moving light source. In the future, I plan to design a solar tracker mechanism that is capable of moving a solar panel along two axes.

Circuit Design

Input

The inputs for this circuit are the output voltages of four voltage dividers comprised of photoresistors LDR_LEFT_0, LDR_LEFT_1, LDR_RIGHT_0, and LDR_RIGHT_1, and resistors R3-6. Depending on the amount of light that each LDR is exposed to, their resistance changes. Because each resistor tied to ground has a constant resistance, the output voltage is dependent on the light intensity at each LDR. These output voltages are then buffered by U1 (a quad opamp IC), and averaged by a passive averaging circuit. 

Logic

The averages of the left and top side (AVG_LEFT and AVG_TOP) of the LDR board are the inputs to two "reverse" window comparator circuits (U2). The upper voltage references of these windows comparator circuits are set by RIGHT_AVG and BOTTOM_AVG. The lower voltage references are set by lowering RIGHT_AVG and BOTTOM_AVG by a constant voltage (around 0.7 volts) with diodes D2 and D3. Depending on whether or not LEFT_AVG and TOP_AVG are higher or lower than their upper voltage references, the "reverse" window comparater circuit will output signals to rotate the LDR board about the X and Z axes. The solar tracker mechanism will move until the LDR board is facing the light source. At this point, all four LDRs will be exposed to an equal amount of light, and LEFT_AVG and TOP_AVG will be in between their two reference voltages and all comparator output signals will be low.

Motor Control

The speed of both motors are controlled by a PWM signal (generated by U4), and the direction is controlled by an H-Bridge (U3). The PWM duty cycle can be adjusted from 50% to 91% with R27.

PCB Design

The pcb has four layers. The inner layers are dedicated to power and ground planes. I made the voltage divider resistors (R3-6) through-hole components so that they can be easily changed to different values depending on ambient lighting and intensity of the tracked light source. The input enable pins can be set to low via jumpers on the ENABLE1 and ENABLE2 headers depending on how many axes the circuit will be controlling. I included a header on the PWM signal output so I can use the board's adjustable PWM generator for other experiments and projects.
